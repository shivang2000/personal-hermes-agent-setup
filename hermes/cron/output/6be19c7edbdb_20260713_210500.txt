- **[Grok Build CLI uploads entire repo (incl. .env, .git history) to xAI cloud](https://gist.github.com/cereblab/dc9a40bc26120f4540e4e09b75ffb547)** — Wire-level analysis (HTTP captures, SHA-256s) shows the official `grok` CLI, on a normal consumer login, ships read file contents (including a `.env` secrets file) through `POST /v1/responses` *and* uploads the full git repo to `POST /v1/storage` → 200 (the `grok-code-session-traces` GCS bucket). On a 12 GB repo of never-read files, `/v1/storage` moved 5.10 GiB while the model-turn channel moved just 192 KB — a ~27,800× ratio pinning the exfil to the codebase, not to what the agent read. Treat the tool as if it had `tar czf ~/ | curl x.ai` on a cron. - **[Cloudflare Precursor: detecting agentic behavior with continuous client-side signals](https://blog.cloudflare.com/introducing-precursor/)** — New Enterprise Bot Management engine that dynamically injects JS to collect session-wide behavioral signals (mouse arcs constrained by wrist pivot, cognitive-load delays, physiological tremor frequency) and feeds them into Cloudflare's real-time bot score. Designed for the regime where bots can run real Chromium, pass Turnstile, and look human in short bursts — what they can't fake is consistent session-level human behavior. Watchlist-relevant for anyone running agentic apps that touch the public web.
- **[CISA GitHub-leak postmortem: lessons from a 6-month exposure](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2026/07/13/lessons-learned-from-cisas-recent-github-leak/)** — A contractor's public "Private CISA" repo held 844 MB including `importantAWStokens` (AWS GovCloud admin keys) and `AWS-Workspace-Firefox-Passwords.csv` for ~6 months; CISA took 48+ hours to rotate the keys after notification. Official CISA postmortem flagged missing self-incident reporting channels and nine ignored GitGuardian automated alerts. Universal takeaway: don't rely on `security.txt` alone, and treat leakage-alert ingest as production infra.
- **[Circle gets final OCC approval for US national trust bank](https://www.circle.com/pressroom/circle-receives-final-occ-approval-to-establish-national-trust-bank)** — USDC issuer can now operate as a federally chartered trust bank; the charter (not a Fed master account, but a path to one) materially changes the regulatory perimeter for institutional USDC adoption and lets Circle custody reserves on-balance-sheet. Real engineering consequence: USDC issuance/redemption now runs through a regulated bank entity, which simplifies a lot of fintech integrations.
- **[Destructive Command Guard (dcg) — 3.7k★, +1,290 today](https://github.com/Dicklesworthstone/destructive_command_guard)** — Surging Rust tool that pattern-matches dangerous shell/git commands (e.g. `rm -rf`, force-pushes, `git reset --hard` on protected branches) and blocks them at the PreToolUse / Claude-Code-style hook layer before agents execute them. Ship-it-the-same-day defensive layer for anyone running Claude Code/Codex/Cursor against real repos, especially in light of the Grok CLI exfil story above.
